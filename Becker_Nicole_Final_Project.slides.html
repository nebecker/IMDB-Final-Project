Part I: Data Cleaning and Descriptive Analysis¶

1. Introduction¶

In this project, I seek to evaluate the historic success of movies and determine which factors have the highest impact on such success. Success in this industry can be quite subjective, as it involves the opinion of others. However, with numerous factors, we can determine trends in movies that end up "successful" and those with worse ratings. For this project, success is related to a high IMDB sccore for the movie. Through this project, I will determine some of the largest predictors of high IMDB scores and high profits for a movie.

1.a. Background Information¶

Research Summary

IMDB began in the 1990s, and it was created to collect information about movies and television series and assemble the information into a database. Now, it contains thousands of entries regarding movies and television, and people can rate each movie, which is aggregated into an IMDB Score. This score indicates how well-liked a movie is by the general public.

In the movie industry, one movie's budget is typically upward of $100 million. Thus, the success of movies in the box office is absolutely crucial to movie producers and the United States movie industry overall. With that, if one could predict the success of a movie prior to its release, it would open up possibilities to grow the movie industry and also advance the customer's entertainment experience.

1.b. Business Objective¶

Project Goals

In this project, I will predict the historic success of movies based on the IMDB score and profits. With this, I will determine what type of characteristics are likely to lead to a successful movie.

This is important because it assists movie makers in creating more favorable movies. This would lead to more profits for movie makers, and perhaps, more profits for the movie industry as a whole. Likewise, the general population would gain more entertainment benefits from movies moving forward.

Regression Summary

For regression, I created a model that would predict IMDB score based off all the variables within this dataset. However, after further analysis, I concluded that selecting a few features of the model to understand it better.. Thereore, I used feature selection to create the regression model. The most important variables for this model are:

  1. Number of critics who reviewed
  2. Duration
  3. Number of users who voted
  4. Number of users who reviewed

Once I created this model, I found the mean square error to be about 78%.

The Equation for this model is as follows:

IMDB Score = 4.86 + 1.03e-03(Number of critics who reviewed) + 1.14e-02(Duration) + 3.44e-06(Number who voted) - 5.57e-04(Number who reviewed)

With this equation, we wil be able to predict an IMDB score for a movie based on its number of critics who reviewed, duration, number who voted, and number who reviewed. This has implications when looking at new movies to be able to identify success earlier.

Classification Summary

For classification, I determined characeteristics for movies within various success categories. The success categories were based on bins of IMDB scores. The bins are as follows:

  • IMDB score < 4 is bad
  • 4 <= IMDB score < 6 is okay
  • 6 <= IMDB score < 8 is good
  • 8 <= IMDB score <= 10 is excecllent

I used a variety of different modeling methods to ensure the classification was easy to interprety and accurate.
